  • Curl of a vector field

    • it measuers the convergence or stability of a vector field

      When the curl = 0 -> The field is conservative (has no curls)

      • A zero curl means also that if you a point in anywhere in the field it will move in the same direction

      Curl is a VECTOR FIELD!

      Insights

      • Consider F to be  the velocity vector field of a fluid flowing

        • The curl(F(a,b,c)) is associated with the rotation of the particles near the point (a,b,c) around an axis points in the direction of curl F

          The magnitude of F = how quickly the particles orbit the axis

          So, If curl=0 the fluid is free from rotation
